Looking for your next read, friends to read with something

We offer items for adults and children. Prices start from $0.25 to $1, and there are Sunday specials. 100% of your purchase goes to the library. We are conveniently located at the Cubberley Community Center. Hours vary by sale room, but all rooms are open 11AM-4PM SAT & 1PM-4PM SUN

Our friendly sales people will help you find treasures in the stacks without sales tax. Need a present? Our gift certificates are available in $5.00 denominations. You can find more information about the Main sale, Children's sale, and Bargain sale by clicking the links

Main sale

Our Main sale offers adult books, CDs, DVDs, records, and magazines. Hardcover books start at $1.00 and paperback books start at $0.50. Main sale hours are 11AM-4PM SAT & 1PM-4PM SUN. The Main room faces East Charleston Rd: find it on our Sale room map. Check out our Bargain sale for even better deals!

If you want first crack on SAT, we use a ticketing system to ensure smooth traffic flow. We hand out up to 2 numbered tickets per person at the Main sale door, starting at 8AM.

Your tickets reserve you places in line when the sale starts at 11AM. You are free to get coffee or check out our Children's and Bargain sales in the meantime. Just get back to the Main room a few minutes before 11AM: you'll forfeit your spot(s) if you're not present. No ticket, no problem! Just join the line and we'll let you in as soon as we can.

We love our early shoppers, but during the first hour of the SAT sale there is a 12 book limit on purchases. You may rejoin the line to enter again. The 12 book limit remains in effect until there is no line

Why the numbers and line and puchase limit? We care about your health and safety, and the size of our Main room limits occupancy to 160 people. Our ticketing system allows early shopping. Our line system and purchase limit allow walk-up customers access. Everybody wins, and so does the library!

Children's sale

Our Children's sale offers books, CDs, DVDs, and toys. Parents and teachers, check us out! We have picture books, youth fiction & non-fiction, and non-Enlish language books. Many prices are $0.50 or $1.00. Children's sale hours are 10AM-4PM SAT & 1PM-4PM SUN. Note that the Children's sale opens 1 hour before the Main sale on Saturday, so be early for the best selection

The Children's sale room faces the Greendell School and San Antonio Rd: find it on our Sale room map. While you're shopping early on SAT, you need to limit your selections to 12 items: just bring them to the cashiers to hold until you are ready to pay for all of your purchases. Not sure about what to buy for that grandchild or teacher? Our gift certificates (available in $5.00 denominations) make great gifts! Check out our Bargain sale for even better deals!

Bargain sale

Check out the Bargain sale for great deals, AND our Sunday specials! All adult hardback books are $1.00, paperbacks are $0.50, and children's books are only $0.25. You have 2 savings options on Sunday. All items are half-price, or you can purchase a $5.00 bag to fill with as many books as you can jam in there. Bargain sale hours are 10AM-4PM SAT & 1PM-4PM SUN. Note that the Bargain sale opens 1 hour before the Main sale on Saturday, so be early for the best selection. The Bargain sale is in Room H2, facing East Charleston Rd: find it on our Sale room map. Not sure about what to buy for that friend or relative? Our gift certificates (available in $5.00 denominations) make great gifts!

Donate

We accept donations from individuals, estates, and businesses. The Palo Alto Library generously donates items removed from their shelves, providing numerous newer materials. You may bring large donations to our main room or contact us to arrange a convenient drop-off time with assistance from our friendly volunteers

About us

Friends of the Palo Alto Library (FOPAL) is a member-supported 501(c)(3) non-profit organization dedicated to supporting the Palo Alto City Library and its users through volunteerism, book sales, and advocacy. Established in 1938, FOPAL has grown with the library community it serves. Our volunteers conduct monthly and online sales of books and other media donated by the community.
