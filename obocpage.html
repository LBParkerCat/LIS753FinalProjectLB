One Book, One Community (OBOC) is a cooperative reading-and-discussion initiative run jointly by the Cook Memorial Public Library District, Indian Trails Public Library District, and Vernon Area Public Library District. Its goal is to encourage dialogue and strengthen community by having residents from across multiple suburbs read the same book during the program period, then come together (in-person or virtually) for book discussions and related events. The program emphasizes inclusivity — events are open to all, regardless of which library you belong to.

At the start of the program, each participating library distributes free copies of the selected book (while supplies last). Over the following weeks, there will be scheduled public events that tie into the book: group discussions, lectures, workshops, and art- or culture-related programs (sometimes loosely connected to the book’s themes, locale, or subject). The season will wrap up with an author event (live or via Zoom) with the book’s author.

Hello Beautiful (Ann Napolitano, 2023)

Drawn into the orbit of a tightknit family upon falling for Julia, the eldest of the four Padavano sisters, William Waters experiences the kind of family solidarity, affection, and sense of belonging he never had with his own dysfunctional parents. William developed an (initially) effective coping strategy during his lonely childhood and devotes his energies toward succeeding in the only place he feels comfortable: the basketball court. College sweethearts, Julia and William marry and begin a life together directed mostly by Julia’s wishes for stability and status; the plan and relationship are derailed by William’s gradual decline into a crippling depression. Julia and William divorce, and William distances himself from their infant daughter, Alice, too.

Relationships between and among William and all of the Padavanos rupture and realign over the ensuing decades as Napolitano spins a saga of familial love, deception, and hope for healing while adeptly highlighting each family member’s unique position in the narrative. Each of the Padavano girls is finely described—there's Julia, who's straightforward and driven; Sylvie, dreamy and romantic; and twins Cecelia (artistic) and Emeline (the sensitive moral compass of the group)—and it is entirely plausible that the girls envision themselves from time to time as the March sisters from Little Women. (Rounding out that parallel is the presence of a dreamy, poetic father and a hardworking, long-suffering mother.)

More subtly, the influence of Walt Whitman is felt throughout the book, from epigraph to end, as characters come to terms with their roles in an evolving universe. As in Napolitano’s recent Dear Edward (2020), heartbreaking circumstances shatter the lives of relatable human characters who are unprepared for the task of building a meaningful life.

--Kirkus Reviews

Little Women (Louisa May Alcott, 1858)

Little Women tells the story of the four March sisters—responsible Meg, independent Jo, gentle Beth, and artistic Amy—as they come of age during the American Civil War. Despite financial hardship, the girls navigate friendship, family struggles, budding ambitions, and first loves. Jo strives to become a writer, Meg dreams of a stable home life, Beth’s kindness shapes the family, and Amy matures into a confident young woman.

As the sisters grow into adulthood, they face loss, changing relationships, and the realization that their paths in life will be different but equally meaningful.

Major themes include the importance of family and sisterhood, the pursuit of personal identity, the tension between societal expectations and individual ambition—especially for women—and the idea that love can take many forms beyond romance.

Little Women in Film

Did you know there are multiple film versions of Little Women? Enhance your reading of Hello Beautiful and Alcott's novel with a movie night. You can find many of these films on DVD and/or Bluray at the library:

  • Little Women (Greta Gerwig, 2019) with Saorise Ronan, Emma Watson, and Timothee Chalamet
  • Little Women (mini-series) (Vanessa Caswill, 2017)
  • Little Women (Gillian Armstrong, 1994) with Winona Ryder and Susan Sarandon
  • Little Women (Mervyn LeRoy, 1949)
  • Little Women (George Cukor, 1933)
  • Little Women (silent films, 1917 and 1918)
